Selected Projects

Neural dynamics of visual processes in challenging visibility conditions

The human brain quickly recognizes objects; however, perceiving objects under difficult conditions is challenging. In our daily life, factors like occlusion, masking, or limited presentation time can render objects invisible or poorly visible. In this project, we used EEG data to decode neural responses and examine rapid object recognition under challenging visibility conditions. We compared brain response decoding for visible and invisible targets in a Rapid Serial Visual Presentation (RSVP) paradigm and found out recurrance is neccesary for recognizing objects in challenging conditions.

Emotion recognition through EEG and ECG data fusion

The fusion of EEG and ECG data has emerged as a promising strategy for comprehending and deciphering human emotions. In our project, we integrated EEG and ECG data using various methods and assessed the performance of EEG data alone, ECG data alone, and the fused EEG and ECG data to determine the most effective modality for emotion recognition. We employed multiple classifiers, including SVM, MLP, and BN, and compared their outcomes. The findings demonstrated that by fusing EEG and ECG data at the decision level, we achieved improved accuracy in recognizing human emotions.
